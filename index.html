Bursa: The Capital of Culture and History

Located in northwestern Turkey, Bursa is a city rich in history and culture. As one of the earliest capitals of the Ottoman Empire, it is home to numerous historical landmarks, such as mosques, tombs, and traditional bazaars. Known for its thermal baths and lush green surroundings, the city is also called "Green Bursa."

Historical Significance

Bursa was the first major capital of the Ottoman Empire, and its heritage can still be seen in its grand mosques and historical buildings. Notable landmarks include the Great Mosque (Ulu Cami), Green Tomb (Yeşil Türbe), and the ancient Silk Bazaar (Koza Han), which reflects the city’s long-standing connection to the Silk Road.

Natural Beauty

Surrounded by mountains, Bursa is famous for its natural beauty. Mount Uludağ, a popular destination for skiing in the winter and hiking in the summer, dominates the landscape. The city's many parks and gardens, along with its thermal spas, make it a peaceful retreat.

Modern Bursa

Today, Bursa is a dynamic industrial and trade center while maintaining its rich cultural heritage. Its combination of modernity and tradition attracts tourists from all over the world. In addition, the city is famous for its unique cuisine, including the well-known Iskender Kebab.
